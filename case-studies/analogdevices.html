Context & Problem

Multiple tools and product lines had diverging interaction models, making evaluation and integration slower and more error-prone. Engineers needed consistent patterns to configure hardware, visualize measurements, and simulate designs across desktop and web.

Discovery & Research

  • Interviewed EEs and FAEs to map critical jobs: discovery, setup, configuration, measurement, reporting
  • Audited desktop + web apps to find duplicative flows and inconsistent idioms
  • Heuristic evaluations and instrumented task walkthroughs to identify friction

Strategy

  • Defined north-star scenarios that spanned Evaluation Studio → Reference Design App → vLab
  • Aligned roadmaps with a shared set of primitives: device setup, data capture, analysis, export
  • Introduced contribution model for patterns via InnerSource to scale adoption

Interaction & Prototyping

  • Created flow libraries and interaction specs for configuration and measurement tasks
  • Established visualization standards for charts, traces, ranges, and units
  • Prototyped critical tasks to validate step reductions with target users

Design System & Accessibility

  • Contributed tokens and components to Harmonic DS; documented behaviors and a11y states
  • Patterns for configuration panels, instrument metaphor components, and data views
  • Accessibility guidance for keyboard, focus, and high-contrast use in labs

Delivery & Collaboration

  • Partnered with engineering to incrementally ship patterns; tracked adoption
  • Facilitated cross-team design reviews and pattern governance
  • Maintained a changelog and migration guidance to reduce churn

Project Overview

At Analog Devices, I've led the UX transformation of critical semiconductor development tools while establishing design leadership practices for a team of 8 product designers. My role spans from hands-on product design to strategic initiatives that shape how electrical engineers interact with ADI's development ecosystem.

The challenge was unique: creating intuitive experiences for highly technical users working with complex hardware evaluation, simulation tools, and development platforms. This required deep understanding of electrical engineering workflows and the ability to translate complex technical requirements into accessible interfaces.

Beyond individual product contributions, I've been instrumental in establishing UX patterns specific to electrical engineering contexts, contributing to ADI's Harmonic Design System, and leading innersource efforts that identify and standardize user experience patterns across the organization.

The Challenge

Semiconductor development tools traditionally prioritized functionality over usability, creating steep learning curves for engineers. Key challenges included:

  • Complex technical workflows with limited UX guidance
  • Fragmented tool ecosystem lacking consistency
  • Engineering-first culture with minimal design awareness
  • Managing diverse team across multiple product domains
  • Establishing UX patterns for specialized engineering contexts

The Solution

I developed a comprehensive UX strategy that balanced technical requirements with user-centered design:

  • Deep user research with electrical engineers across domains
  • Established design system contributions and patterns
  • Built design leadership practices for distributed team
  • Created engineering-specific UX methodologies
  • Led innersource initiatives for UX pattern identification

Key Learnings

Engineering Culture Requires Adapted UX Methods: Traditional UX approaches needed modification for highly technical audiences. Engineers value precision, efficiency, and logical information architecture over purely aesthetic considerations.

Design Systems in Technical Contexts Need Domain Expertise: Contributing to design systems for semiconductor tools required deep understanding of engineering workflows and technical constraints beyond traditional UI patterns.

Team Leadership in Technical Organizations Requires Translation: Successfully managing designers in engineering-first culture required constant translation between design value and technical business outcomes.
