Project Overview

XWiki represented my most significant design challenge: transforming a complex open source knowledge management platform into an intuitive enterprise solution while maintaining the community-driven innovation that made it special.

Over 10 years, I led the complete UX transformation of XWiki Enterprise, designing everything from core platform features to advanced collaboration tools. The challenge wasn't just about improving usability—it was about creating a scalable design system that could serve both open source contributors and enterprise customers with vastly different needs.

My role evolved from Interaction Designer to Lead Interaction Designer, ultimately becoming responsible for orchestrating user experience across all XWiki products and helping the company achieve 2x revenue growth while serving over 10 million users worldwide.

The Challenge

XWiki faced a critical paradox: the platform's power came from its flexibility and openness, but this same complexity created barriers for mainstream enterprise adoption. Key challenges included:

  • Steep learning curve for non-technical users
  • Inconsistent UX across 50+ features and extensions
  • Lack of enterprise-grade onboarding and workflows
  • Community-driven development without design consistency
  • Need to balance open source accessibility with commercial viability

The Solution

I developed a comprehensive design strategy that addressed both community and enterprise needs through:

  • Scalable design system serving both audiences
  • Progressive disclosure for complex functionality
  • Community-driven design process with enterprise validation
  • Consistent interaction patterns across all features
  • Strategic feature prioritization based on user research

Design Process

1

Community Research & Discovery

Conducted extensive user research with both open source contributors and enterprise users to understand different mental models, workflows, and pain points. Used forums, surveys, and direct interviews to map user journeys.

2

Information Architecture Redesign

Restructured the platform's information architecture to create clear pathways for different user types while maintaining the flexibility that power users required.

3

Design System Creation

Built a comprehensive design system that could scale across community-contributed extensions while maintaining visual and interaction consistency. Created guidelines for both internal teams and external contributors.

4

Feature-by-Feature Transformation

Systematically redesigned core features including Application Within Minutes, Extension Manager, Search, Rights Management, Activity Stream, Administration, and Workspaces—each with extensive prototyping and user testing.

5

Enterprise Integration & Validation

Worked directly with enterprise clients like Amazon and EMC to validate designs against real-world enterprise workflows and requirements, iterating based on actual deployment feedback.

Key Learnings

Community-Driven Design Requires Structure: While community input was invaluable, successful scaling required establishing clear design principles and guidelines that contributors could follow.

Enterprise Adoption Needs Simplified Onboarding: Even powerful platforms fail without intuitive first-time experiences. Creating progressive disclosure patterns was crucial for enterprise success.

Open Source Success Translates to Commercial Value: By improving the open source experience first, we created a stronger foundation for enterprise offerings, resulting in authentic community-driven growth.
